What is the Nvidia GeForce GTX 1080?


The GTX 1080 is Nvidia's latest top-end graphics card, ready to take on this year's two-pronged assault of VR and 4K gaming. Its performance this year makes it by far the most powerful consumer level graphics card (ignoring the outrageous Titan X), and netted it our Graphics Card of the Year award at our 2016 Trusted Reviews Awards ceremony. Read on to find out how it achieved this.

If you’re wondering why Nvidia's made such an enormous song and dance out of a consumer graphics card launch, it’s because the company spent ‘billions’ on getting its new architecture to market and now has to make the numbers add up. Both financially and in GPU performance.

Since my original review of the GTX 1080, a lot’s changed. Nvidia has launched two more GPUs in the form of the GTX 1070 and GTX 1060, fully fleshing out its range of graphics cards.

While the performance of the GTX 1080 isn’t in doubt – and it’s still by far the most powerful consumer card on the market – you definitely don’t need to spend the £600+ on it if your ambitions for playing games don’t extend to the world of 4K and VR.

The 8GB, £400 GTX 1070 now offers the best compromise of performance and price if you’re after 1440p gameplay, and is also a handy performer with VR.

Meanwhile, the GTX 1060 is the lowest-priced Nvidia GPU you can buy if you want to get into the world of VR, although I’d still recommend you don’t drop below the 1070 to ensure future titles will play nicely.
